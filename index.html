Working Principle

This clock measures time based on the speed of light and the size of the universe. Instead of using Earth's rotation, it calculates how long a single particle of light (a photon) takes to travel the average distance between two galaxies.

Because our universe is constantly stretching and expanding, that distance gets longer in every new cycle—or "Epoch"—meaning the photon has farther to travel and the clock's "ticks" gradually stretch out.

However, this cosmic expansion is so incredibly subtle that the clock would have to run constantly for over a million years before a human would notice even a single 1-second delay in its timing.

Definitions

  • EPOCH: A normalized factor obtained from average galactic separation and photon travel time. More info in "Theoretical Framework".
  • INTEGER (100.00): Counts as 1% of the Epoch.
  • COUNTER (0.01): Counts as 1% of the Integer.
